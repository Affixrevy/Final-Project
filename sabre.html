Sabre

How does Sabre work?

Sabre is one of the three modern fencing weapons. It is different than the other two fencing weapons, the foil and épée, as it is possibel to score by slashing the opponent with the edge of the blade. As a concequence sabre movements and attacks are extreamly fast. Like the foil but unlike the épée, sabre employes the right-of-way/priority to determine who gets the touch.

The Sabre blade is usually I or V shaped unlike the rectangular blade of the foil and is not as stiff than the épée. Adult sized blades are 88 cm in length with a maximum weight of 500g. Target area for Sabre fencers consists of the torso, above the waist, including the arms and hands but excluding both hands (all areas hilightd red in photo). Because touches are scored by slashing in Sabre, if there is any contact between the blade and target area, a point is scored.
